### 2. Vision Statement

To become the most trusted, developer-friendly, and enterprise-ready Authentication & Authorization platform globally — empowering every organization, from a two-person startup to a Fortune 500 enterprise, to secure every identity seamlessly.

---

### 3. Mission Statement

Qeet ID exists to eliminate the complexity of identity management. We make authentication simple for developers, powerful for enterprises, and seamless for end users — all on one unified, secure, and scalable platform.

---

### 4. Strategic Business Goals

---

### Goal 1 — Build & Launch a Market-Ready MVP

**Statement:**
Design, develop, and launch Qeet ID MVP within 15 months with core authentication, authorization, and developer experience capabilities that compete directly with Auth0 and Okta.

**Why it matters:**
Speed to market is critical in the identity space. Every month of delay is market share lost to established players. A well-scoped MVP with exceptional developer experience will drive organic adoption faster than a feature-bloated delayed launch.

**Owner:** CTO + Product Manager

---

### Goal 2 — Achieve Developer-First Market Adoption

**Statement:**
Establish Qeet ID as the go-to authentication platform for developers and startups through a generous free tier, world-class SDKs, comprehensive documentation, and an active developer community.

**Why it matters:**
Auth0 and Firebase won by winning developers first. Enterprises follow where developers lead. If Qeet ID becomes the default recommendation in developer communities, enterprise deals follow naturally.

**Owner:** Product Manager + Developer Relations

---

### Goal 3 — Capture Enterprise Customers

**Statement:**
Onboard a minimum of 5 enterprise pilot customers before public launch and grow to 50 paying enterprise customers within 12 months of launch.

**Why it matters:**
Enterprise customers drive the majority of revenue in the identity space. One enterprise deal can be worth more than 10,000 free tier developer accounts. Early enterprise pilots also provide critical feedback to harden the platform.

**Owner:** CTO + Enterprise Sales

---

### Goal 4 — Achieve Compliance & Security Certification

**Statement:**
Obtain SOC 2 Type I before production launch and SOC 2 Type II within 12 months of launch. Maintain full GDPR compliance from Day 1.

**Why it matters:**
In the authentication space, trust is the product. No enterprise will integrate an identity platform without verified compliance certifications. Security is not a feature — it is the foundation.

**Owner:** Security Architect + Compliance Officer

---

### Goal 5 — Build a Scalable, Reliable Infrastructure

**Statement:**
Design and deploy infrastructure capable of supporting 1 million MAUs by Year 2, with a minimum uptime SLA of 99.9% from launch and 99.99% by Year 2.

**Why it matters:**
Authentication is mission-critical infrastructure. A single outage means every customer's users cannot log in. Reliability is non-negotiable and directly tied to customer trust and retention.

**Owner:** CTO + DevOps / SRE Team

---

### Goal 6 — Generate Sustainable Revenue

**Statement:**
Generate first revenue within 3 months of public launch, achieve $1M ARR within 12 months of launch, and reach $5M ARR by end of Year 2.

**Why it matters:**
Qeet ID must demonstrate financial viability as a standalone Qeet Group subsidiary. Revenue validates the business model and funds continued product development and team growth.

**Owner:** Product Manager + Finance + Sales

---

### Goal 7 — Establish Qeet ID Brand in the Market

**Statement:**
Within 12 months of launch, establish Qeet ID as a recognized name in the authentication and identity space through developer community presence, content marketing, conference presence, and strategic partnerships.

**Why it matters:**
Brand recognition directly drives inbound leads, developer adoption, and enterprise trust. In a crowded market, being known and trusted is as important as the product itself.

**Owner:** Marketing + Developer Relations

### 9. Assumptions & Constraints

**Assumptions:**

- Qeet Group funding is secured before Phase 2 begins
- Full team of 15+ is hired and onboarded before development starts
- Cloud infrastructure costs are within the approved budget
- Competitor landscape remains consistent during the build period
- Developer community adoption follows a bottom-up growth model

**Constraints:**

- SOC 2 Type I must be achieved before production launch — non-negotiable
- GDPR compliance is a hard requirement at launch
- ARR targets are subject to market conditions and pricing validation
- KPI targets will be re-baselined after 3 months of real production data
